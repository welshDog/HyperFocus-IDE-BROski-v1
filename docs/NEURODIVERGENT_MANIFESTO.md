# HyperCode: A Neurodivergent-First Way to Code

**For the ADHD, Dyslexic, and Autistic Coders Who Think Differently.**

Traditional IDEs are designed for linear thinkers. They demand rote memorization, endless context switching, and sustained attention on minutiae—the very things that can paralyze a neurodivergent brain.

**HyperCode is different.** It is not just an IDE; it is a cognitive prosthetic. It is an **AI-powered development environment** built from the ground up to align with how we actually think: visually, creatively, and in bursts of hyperfocus.

## The Problem: The "Wall of Text"

If you have dyslexia, a terminal error log isn't just information; it's a visual assault. If you have ADHD, switching from your code to documentation to a terminal to Slack is a guaranteed way to lose your "flow state" for the next hour.

We spend 80% of our energy fighting the environment and only 20% solving the problem.

## The Solution: HyperSwarm Agent Crew

HyperCode replaces the lonely struggle with a **collaborative swarm of AI agents**. You are no longer a solo coder trying to juggle eight different hats (frontend, backend, DevOps, QA). You are the **Mission Commander**.

### 1. Reduce Context Switching
Instead of tab-switching to Google or Stack Overflow, you delegate research to the **Researcher Agent**. You stay in your editor. The answer comes to you.

### 2. Visual Control (Mission Control)
Our dashboard isn't a list of files. It's a **Visual Cortex**—a real-time graph showing your agents, their status, and the flow of data. You can *see* the work happening. It makes the abstract concrete.

### 3. Chunking by Default
The **Project Strategist Agent** automatically breaks down vague, overwhelming goals ("Build a habit tracker") into small, actionable steps. It handles the executive function tax so you don't have to.

### 4. Fearless Exploration
The **Phoenix (DevOps) Agent** watches your back. If you break the build, it auto-detects the crash and proposes a fix. You can experiment without the paralyzing fear of "destroying everything."

## Built for Neurodiversity

*   **Dyslexia-Friendly**: We prioritize visual cues, clear typography, and voice-to-text interfaces.
*   **ADHD-Friendly**: We automate the boring stuff (scaffolding, testing, git commits) to keep your dopamine high.
*   **Autism-Friendly**: We enforce strict, predictable patterns (HDVC Standards) so you never have to guess "how" to write code—only "what" to build.
